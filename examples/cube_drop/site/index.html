sim2sim render benchmark — 2 scenes, 1 env

Two single-environment scenes rendered by six physics engines, rasterized and path-traced natively: Scene 1 a tilted cube dropped onto a plane, and Scene 2 the real LeRobot bipedal platform (STL meshes, 12 DOF) holding a stance with its built-in PD servos. This measures per-frame render/readback overhead — not the massively-parallel multi-env workloads the GPU engines (Genesis, mjlab, Isaac, Nexus) are designed for. Generated by examples/cube_drop/benchmark.py · site by make_site.py.

Results

Raster fps = per-frame loop (physics + render) with frames staying on the GPU — no full-frame readback (rows not re-measured on that basis are marked). Native-RT rows are each engine path-tracing its own scene. Not apples-to-apples across resolutions. On the RTX 5090 desktop the three portable backends (MuJoCo, mjlab, Genesis) were re-measured (median of 3, machine idle), and the Nexus raster + native-RT rows were freshly measured on the 5090 from a full source build of haixuanTao's headless-render Nexus + kiss3d (offscreen WebGPU, no display). The RT row is far faster than the laptop's because that build includes kiss3d's transform-only TLAS fast path, which drops the per-frame BLAS re-bake (242 ms/frame on the laptop → 2.2 ms here).

Nexus numbers use a patched kiss3d: its read_pixels converted pixels by indexing uncached mapped GPU memory per byte (~99 ms/frame at 640×480); converting from a cached row copy + reusing the staging buffer cut readback to ~5 ms (18×) — see kiss3d #397 (readback fix), nexus #7 (frame export) and nexus #8 (Python ray tracing).

Isaac Sim on the desktop: Isaac Sim 5.x's Omniverse RTX renderer crashes on driver 595.71.05 (R590/595-branch incompatibility — this box rendered fine on driver 580 until the 2026-07-10 upgrade), so the 5.x raster/RT rows exist only on the laptop panel; the desktop Scene-3 and Startup rows run headless physics via Isaac Lab's AppLauncher kit experience, which skips the crashing plugins. Isaac Sim 6.0.1.0 fixes the renderer on this driver (verified 2026-07-15: full RTX experience boots in 11 s and renders camera pixels; separate venv at ~/rt_build/isaac6-venv, not yet paired with Isaac Lab) — desktop Isaac render rows are now possible and pending a port of the render demos to 6.0. Genesis native ray tracing on the desktop runs via a from-source LuisaRender build (CUDA backend, pip-wheel CUDA 12.9 toolchain).
